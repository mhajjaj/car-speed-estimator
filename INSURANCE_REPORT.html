6. Why the Finding Is Robust

  • Three independent methods agree within 1.1% (56.8–57.4 km/h).
  • No single assumption is critical. Changing height or tilt across their entire plausible ranges still yields speeds at or above the limit.
  • Pleiotropic factors: Random errors in timing, pixel tracking, and perspective mapping are uncorrelated — they do not all bias in the same direction.
  • The 95% CI (51.0–63.3) does not include 40 km/h. The probability that the true speed was at or below the limit is negligible.

7. Limitations & Appropriate Use

  • This is a computer-vision estimation, not a certified speed-gun measurement. It is suitable for: insurance claim support, accident reconstruction, and relative-liability assessment.
  • Absolute accuracy is estimated at ±10% (±5.7 km/h); the finding of > 40 km/h is robust even within this tolerance.
  • The estimate assumes a flat road and constant speed during the 3–16 s window. Gradual acceleration/deceleration would widen the confidence interval but not shift the mean below the limit.
  • Legal enforcement actions should rely on certified equipment (radar / LIDAR / GPS).
